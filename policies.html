Privacy Policy

Purpose of this Policy

This privacy policy applies to Al Falah Educational Centre (www.alfalahslmcc.co.uk) and outlines how we protect and manage the privacy of our users. We are committed to safeguarding your personal information and being transparent about our practices.

Our Website

We take a proactive approach to user privacy and ensure necessary steps are taken to protect the privacy of our users throughout their experience on our website. Our practices comply with all UK national laws and requirements for user privacy.

Use of Cookies

Our website uses cookies to enhance user experience. Cookies are small files saved to your computer's hard drive that track and store information about your interactions with our website, allowing us to provide a more tailored experience.

You can control cookie settings through your web browser's security settings. Our website uses Google Analytics to monitor visitor usage, which helps us understand how our site is used. Google Analytics uses cookies but does not store, save, or collect personal information.

Contact & Communication

When you contact us through our website, you provide personal details at your discretion. We keep this information private and secure in accordance with data protection regulations. We use this information to respond to your queries and provide information about our services, but only with your explicit permission.

Your Data Protection Rights

You have rights regarding your personal information, including:

  • The right to access your personal data
  • The right to correct inaccurate data
  • The right to request deletion of your data
  • The right to restrict or object to processing
  • The right to data portability

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details in our Contact section.

Safeguarding Policy

Al Falah Educational Centre acknowledges our duty of care to safeguard and promote the welfare of children and is committed to ensuring our safeguarding practices reflect statutory responsibilities, government guidance, and best practice standards.

Our Commitment

We recognise that the welfare and interests of children are paramount in all circumstances. We aim to ensure that all children, regardless of age, ability, gender, race, religion, or background, have a positive and enjoyable experience in a safe, child-centred environment.

Key Principles

  • Prioritising the safety and wellbeing of children and young people
  • Ensuring all staff and volunteers understand their safeguarding responsibilities
  • Providing appropriate training to recognise and respond to safeguarding concerns
  • Taking appropriate action when incidents or concerns arise
  • Maintaining confidential and accurate records of safeguarding concerns
  • Preventing unsuitable individuals from working with children

Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually and updated in response to changes in legislation, government guidance, or as required by regulatory bodies.

Complaints Policy

We welcome all complaints as opportunities to improve our services. Our policy is to investigate complaints fully and resolve them wherever possible.

What is a Complaint?

A complaint is an expression of dissatisfaction about our policies or actions, whether justified or not. Complaints can be received verbally, by phone, email, or in writing.

Complaints Procedure

Our complaints procedure has four stages:

  1. Stage One: Resolution by the relevant staff member
  2. Stage Two: Escalation to management if not resolved
  3. Stage Three: Review by Board of Trustees
  4. External Stage: Complaint to relevant regulatory body

Speakers & Imams Policy

We occasionally invite speakers and Imams from our wider community to deliver talks and Friday sermons, enriching our community's knowledge and understanding of Islam.

Guidelines for Speakers

All speakers at Al Falah Educational Centre must adhere to the following guidelines:

  • Deliver talks in English or a universally understood language
  • Refrain from soliciting donations or fundraising during events
  • Avoid publicising other organisations or distributing literature
  • Respect different schools of Islamic thought and avoid divisive discussions
  • Approach sensitive topics with care and wisdom
  • Refrain from promoting illegal activities or discrimination
  • Align with Islamic values, British values, and our centre's ethos

Volunteers Policy

We value the contributions of volunteers who help build and run our centre. This policy outlines our approach to working with volunteers.

Volunteer Status

A volunteer gives their time, skills, and experience without expectation of financial reward. Volunteers are not employees and do not have employment contracts.

Recruitment & Training

  • Volunteers complete an application form
  • References and relevant checks are conducted
  • Volunteers receive appropriate training for their role
  • Health and safety training is provided

Volunteer Support

  • Each volunteer has a designated supervisor
  • Expenses are reimbursed according to our policy
  • Insurance coverage is provided
  • Regular reviews and support sessions

Confidentiality & Conduct

Volunteers are expected to maintain confidentiality and comply with all centre policies. We provide a safe and supportive environment for all volunteers.

Volunteer Drivers: Volunteers who transport equipment or people must have valid driving licenses and appropriate insurance. All motoring incidents must be reported to the centre.
